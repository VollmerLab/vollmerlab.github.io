    <section class="section">

        <h2 class="section-title">Research Areas</h2>

        <ul class="research-list">

            <li class="research-item">

                <h3>Coral Resilience Genetics</h3>

                <p>Understanding the genetic basis of coral disease resistance and thermal tolerance. We identify genes and genomic regions that help corals survive diseases like White Band Disease and adapt to warming oceans.</p>

            </li>

            

            <li class="research-item">

                <h3>Coral Microbiome Characterization</h3>

                <p>Studying coral-associated microbial communities to distinguish beneficial symbionts from harmful pathogens. We use metagenomics to understand how diverse microbiomes influence coral health and disease susceptibility.</p>

            </li>

            

            <li class="research-item">

                <h3>Conservation Solutions</h3>

                <p>Translating genomic discoveries into practical conservation applications. We work with coral restoration organizations to select disease-resistant genotypes and develop evidence-based strategies for reef restoration.</p>

            </li>

        </ul>

    </section>


    <section class="section">

        <h2 class="section-title">Recent Publications</h2>

        

        <div class="research-item">

            <p><strong>Vollmer, S. V.</strong>, Selwyn, J. D., Despard, B. A., & Roesel, C. L. (2023). Genomic signatures of disease resistance in endangered staghorn corals. <em>Science</em>, <em>381</em>(6665), 1451-1454. DOI:10.1126/science.adi3601</p>

            <p style="color: #666; font-size: 0.9rem; margin-top: 0.5rem; font-style: italic;">Identified 10 genomic regions and 73 SNPs associated with white band disease resistance in Caribbean staghorn corals. Polygenic scores can predict disease resistance and be applied to improving coral restoration efforts.</p>

        </div>


        <div class="research-item">

            <p>Selwyn, JD, Despard, BA, Vollmer, MV, Trytten EC, <strong>Vollmer, SV</strong> (2024) Machine learning based identification of putative coral pathogens in endangered Caribbean staghorn coral. <em>Environmental Microbiology</em> 26 (9) e16700 https://doi.org/10.1111/1462-2920.16700</p>

            <p style="color: #666; font-size: 0.9rem; margin-top: 0.5rem; font-style: italic;">Applied machine learning approaches to identify potential coral pathogens, advancing understanding of white band disease etiology in endangered Caribbean staghorn corals.</p>

        </div>


        <div class="research-item">

            <p>Selwyn, J. D., Despard, B. A., Galvan-Dubois, K. A., Trytten, E. C., & <strong>Vollmer, S. V.</strong> (2025). Antibiotic pretreatment inhibits white band disease infection by suppressing the bacterial pathobiome. <em>Frontiers in Marine Science</em>, <em>12</em>, 1491476.</p>

            <p style="color: #666; font-size: 0.9rem; margin-top: 0.5rem; font-style: italic;">Prophylactic antibiotic treatment reduced white band disease infection rates by 30 percentage points by suppressing Vibrio species and other opportunistic pathogens in coral microbiomes.</p>

        </div>


        <div class="research-item">

            <p>Despard, B.A., Selwyn, J.D., Shupp, A.N. and <strong>Vollmer, S.V.</strong> (2025), A Network Approach to White Band Disease Challenged Staghorn Coral <em>Acropora cervicornis</em> microRNAs and Their Targets. <em>Ecol Evol</em>, 15: e71351. https://doi.org/10.1002/ece3.71351</p>

            <p style="color: #666; font-size: 0.9rem; margin-top: 0.5rem; font-style: italic;">Used network analysis to identify microRNA-target interactions in disease-challenged staghorn corals, revealing post-transcriptional gene regulation mechanisms during white band disease infection.</p>

        </div>


        <div class="research-item" style="border-bottom: none; padding-bottom: 0; margin-bottom: 0;">

            <p>Selwyn, J. D., & <strong>Vollmer, S. V.</strong> (2023). Whole genome assembly and annotation of the endangered Caribbean coral <em>Acropora cervicornis</em>. <em>G3 Genes|Genomes|Genetics</em>, Volume 13, Issue 12, December 2023, jkad232, https://doi.org/10.1093/g3journal/jkad232</p>

            <p style="color: #666; font-size: 0.9rem; margin-top: 0.5rem; font-style: italic;">Assembled and annotated the first high-quality reference genome for endangered staghorn coral, providing a crucial genomic resource for coral conservation and disease resistance research.</p>

        </div>
